Maracay, Venezuela.

Maracay, known as the "Garden City", is the capital of the Aragua state in Venezuela. Located near the coast of the Caribbean Sea, this city is famous for its pleasant climate, extensive parks and gardens, and rich cultural history.
Maracay is home to the Henri Pittier National Park, one of the most important natural areas in the country, where a great diversity of flora and fauna can be found. In addition, it is a significant industrial and military center, with an economy based on manufacturing and defense. The city is also known for its recreational activities, such as Choroní Beach, and local festivities that reflect the vibrant Venezuelan spirit.
